# Prompts para Mapas — "Herederos de las Ruinas"

Set de 10 prompts (1 mapa general + 9 regionales) pensados para generar una serie de mapas consistentes entre sí, alineados con `Geografia_del_Mundo.md` (3 continentes habitados, 22 inhabitados, sistema de portales) y `Fauna_y_Flora.md` (Anclados, bestiario). Usa la **Guía de estilo compartida** como base y pégala (o resúmela) junto con cada prompt regional para mantener coherencia visual.

---

## Guía de estilo compartida (incluir/repetir en cada prompt)

"Fantasy atlas map, hand-drawn cartography on aged parchment, ink linework with watercolor wash, ornate compass rose, weathered edges, sepia base tones with regional color accents, fine decorative border, top-down orthographic projection, no modern elements (steam-age technology only: railways, dirigibles, riverboats — no electricity). Consistent icon language across the whole atlas: small fortified square = one of the 10 great cities of a habited continent, tower/spire with a small sigil = a branch (sucursal) of one of the 3 Institutions (Instituto de las Ruinas, Biblioteca Infinita, Imperio Eterno), stylized giant tree/landmark = an Anclado (glowing for Ascendido, dark/cracked for Corrompido), spiral glyph = zona de Eco errático o anomalía, dotted line with a small ring gate icon = ruta de portal entre continentes, sea-serpent or colossal-beast silhouette = fauna colosal, faint cracked-ruin icon = ruina maldita (intento fallido de 11ª/12ª ciudad)."

## Mapa 0 — Mapa General / Planetario

"Massive planetary-scale fantasy map, [Guía de estilo compartida]. The 3 known habited continents — Avar (el Vertical sin Jerarquía, ruled by the Instituto de las Ruinas), Sylvaris (el Bosque Vertical, ruled by the Biblioteca Infinita), Nórdkar (el Invierno de Acero, ruled by the Imperio Eterno) — appear as three separate landmasses, each occupying a small fraction of the map, with no land or sea route connecting them. Between them, draw faint dotted ring-gate icons marking the **sistema de portales** (Geografia_del_Mundo.md sección 3) as the only connection.

Scattered across the rest of the map, render the 22 uninhabited continents as varied outlines, 6 of them labeled with name and a matching visual cue (a sleeping colossal beast shape for 'El Durmiente', jagged crystalline shards for 'Los Filos de Cristal', looping ghost-architecture for 'El Eco en Bucle', a cracked hourglass glyph for 'El Reloj Roto', a dense swarm of beast silhouettes for 'Las Fauces sin Fin', a hazy choking-mist overlay for 'El Aliento que Asfixia') and a small station-icon (tiny fortified outpost, much smaller than the great cities) marking their permanent Trazador settlement. The remaining 16 continents are drawn only in bare outline with 'tierras sin nombre' labels, 10 of them additionally marked 'jamás cartografiado' with a warning glyph.

Beyond all the continents, vast uncharted ocean with faint sea-monster silhouettes half-visible beneath the waves, and two enormous turtle-shaped silhouettes (the Tortugas-León) drawn barely visible at the far edges of the map — rendered with extreme subtlety, almost like a watermark, since their existence is a closely guarded secret.

Add a corner cartouche titled 'Herederos de las Ruinas — Mapa del Mundo Conocido' with a small inset/locator box reinforcing how vast the unexplored world is relative to the 3 habited continents, plus the shared icon-legend key."

## Mapa 1 — Avar, el Vertical sin Jerarquía

"[Guía de estilo compartida]. Detailed map of Avar: kilometers-tall mountain ranges and climate bands running pole to pole — frozen peaks, scorching desert, deep canyons, all visible across the same landmass.

- Mark the **10 great cities**, each on a neutral-altitude plateau (never in the ice, the desert, the peak, or the valley floor) — visually distinct islands of stability connected by perilous dotted travel-routes that each cross a different climate extreme.
- Mark a crown-and-tower icon near the largest city for the **Instituto de las Ruinas**, the Institution that governs this continent, plus small unlabeled branch-tower icons for the Biblioteca Infinita and the Imperio Eterno near one or two other cities.
- Mark Anclados as glowing/dark landmark icons: **El Árbol del Mundo** (colossal tree on one of the neutral plateaus, glowing Ascendido aura, scholar-camps beneath its roots), **El Cauce Verde** (a moving prairie shown mid-migration across a climate band, leaving a visible trail), **El Devorador de Arena** (colossal semi-buried carnivorous plant in the desert band, false oasis with small offering-shrines around it), **El Roble del Primer Juramento** (ancient oak at a temperate-band crossroads, ritual grove of travelers/merchants), **El Loto de Cassyr** (glowing lotus flowers on a frozen plateau lake, small Biblioteca Infinita study-garden around it).
- Mark bestiary creatures as small silhouette icons along the travel-routes: **El Centinela de Cien Ojos** (owl, near the Loto de Cassyr), **El Padre de Piedra** (gorilla, lower mountain slopes), **El Primogénito Blanco** (unicorn, near the Roble del Primer Juramento), **El Heraldo de las Cumbres** (griffon, highest peaks, with small domesticated-mount icons nearby), **La Serpiente de los Mil Anillos** (faint shifting-dune trail in the desert band, implying a colossal buried serpent).
- Faint cracked-ruin icons scattered in the wilderness between cities, marking failed attempts at an 11th/12th city.
- Color accent: a wide gradient across the climate bands — icy blue-white, scorching ochre/red, deep canyon grays, temperate green at the plateaus."

## Mapa 2 — Sylvaris, el Bosque Vertical

"[Guía de estilo compartida]. Detailed map of Sylvaris: a colossal forest climbing mountain ranges in layers, with sea-scale freshwater rivers and lakes threading through it.

- Mark the **10 great cities**, each beside a great lake or river confluence — the only clearings stable and open enough in an otherwise impenetrable forest. Connect them with river/lake travel-routes (no rail icon needed here — boats and barges dominate) rather than dotted overland routes.
- Mark a crown-and-tower icon near the largest riverside city for the **Biblioteca Infinita**, the Institution that governs this continent, plus small unlabeled branch-tower icons for the Instituto de las Ruinas and the Imperio Eterno at one or two other cities.
- Mark Anclados: **La Madre de Niebla** (colossal coral/algae mass over a foggy stretch of river basin, Corrompido), **El Manglar Eterno** (lush stable mangrove ecosystem at a river confluence, Ascendido), **El Sauce de las Voces** (corrupted willow deep in an interior swamp, faint whisper-lines emanating from it), **El Bosque que Camina** (a forest visibly mid-stride at the edge of uncharted wilderness, trees angled as if fleeing something), **El Faro Viviente** (glowing bioluminescent tree marking a safe night channel on a great lake shore).
- Mark bestiary creatures: **La Ballena de Sal** (whale-shape gliding through one of the sea-scale lakes), **El Tejedor de Abismos** (giant squid silhouette in the deepest lake waters, bioluminescent glow).
- Faint cracked-ruin icons in the deep, unmapped forest between river basins.
- Color accent: deep greens and blue-greens, silver river-glints, golden dappled light where canopy breaks over the cities."

## Mapa 3 — Nórdkar, el Invierno de Acero

"[Guía de estilo compartida]. Detailed map of Nórdkar: eternal winter, white mountains blending into the sky, and a forest of Acero-Vivo (metal-hard living wood) as tall as the peaks themselves.

- Mark the **10 great cities**, each in one of the few valleys where the cold is 'only' extreme and not instantly lethal — each one competing for access to the nearest Acero-Vivo groves.
- Mark a crown-and-tower icon (with a hereditary-seal motif, distinct from the other two Institutions) near the capital valley-city for the **Imperio Eterno**, the Institution that governs this continent, plus small unlabeled branch-tower icons for the Instituto de las Ruinas and the Biblioteca Infinita at one or two other cities. Add a distinctly different icon (a guild-hammer sigil) marking the timber/forge guild halls that function as the continent's functional aristocracy alongside the Imperio.
- Mark Anclados: **El Corazón de la Tierra** (an incandescent root glowing beneath the ice, warming a livable valley, Ascendido), **El Pilar Blanco** (a living ice-tree that never melts, on one mountain face, temperate micro-valley around it), **La Espina Negra** (a dark volcanic-crystal tree on the opposite face, jagged crystal growths), **La Zarza de Hierro** (a thornbush fused with Acero-Vivo veins in a deep mining zone, dark glow), **El Cedro de las Siete Mareas** (an ancient cedar with seven visible ring-layers, in a low valley accessible only in the least lethal season).
- Mark bestiary creatures: **El Guía de Escarcha** (elk leading a migrating herd along a mountain pass), **El Ciervo de Cristal** (deer with crystalline antlers near the highest peaks).
- Faint cracked-ruin icons scattered in the deep snowfields between valleys.
- Color accent: whites, icy blues, dark grays/blacks for corrupted crystal zones, warm ember-orange glow around the Corazón de la Tierra valley."

## Mapa 4 — Continente VIII, El Durmiente (inhabitado, con estación)

"[Guía de estilo compartida]. Detailed map of a continent that is, in fact, the back/shell of a colossal sleeping beast — render the entire landmass with visible scale-like or shell-like terrain texture, subtly suggesting a living surface beneath ordinary-looking hills and coastline.

- Mark a single small permanent station (tiny fortified outpost, far smaller than a great city) led by a Trazador of at least Abismo rank — emphasize its smallness against the vastness of the sleeping body.
- Mark **Caparazón del Mundo** itself as the landmark — not a separate icon but the continent's outline, with a faint glowing seam or rune-line suggesting where the 'shell' might one day move.
- Add subtle warning glyphs around the coastline (spiral Eco glyph, faint cracked-ruin icon) hinting this was once explored further but abandoned.
- Color accent: muted earthy tones that could be mistaken for ordinary terrain, with faint bioluminescent green seams suggesting something alive underneath."

## Mapa 5 — Continente IX, Los Filos de Cristal (inhabitado, con estación)

"[Guía de estilo compartida]. Detailed map of a continent covered in fields of crystallized echo-fragments — sharp, glinting shard formations covering most of the terrain, remnants of a massive historical death-event now solidified into a weaponized landscape.

- Mark a single small permanent station near the safest edge of the continent, well clear of the densest shard fields.
- Mark **La Espiral de Cuarzo** (a colossal spiral lichen growing over the shards, Ascendido glow) near the station.
- Mark **Wyrm de los Filos de Cristal** (a dragon silhouette coiled in a deep shard-crater at the continent's center, hoarding glinting object-icons in its lair).
- Render the shard fields with a dangerous, glittering ink-wash texture distinct from normal terrain — almost like cracked glass over the whole landmass.
- Color accent: pale crystalline whites and blues, sharp glints of refracted light, deep shadow in the central crater."

## Mapa 6 — Continente VI, El Eco en Bucle (inhabitado, con estación)

"[Guía de estilo compartida]. Detailed map of a continent that was once habited and collapsed — render it as a ghost-double image: faint translucent overlays of ruined city-shapes repeating at the edges of the main terrain, as if the same moment keeps replaying.

- Mark the single small permanent station built into **El Jardín de Cenizo** (an Ascendido Anclado whose roots visibly preserve half-buried artifacts and ruin-fragments without damaging them).
- Mark **La Quimera de Fragmentos** as a roaming creature-icon near the ruin fields — a mismatched, artificial-looking beast silhouette stitched from animal and object shapes.
- Add several faint, looping ghost-ruin icons (more numerous and more deliberately 'repeating' than the standard cracked-ruin icon used elsewhere) to visually distinguish this continent's specific brand of danger.
- Color accent: faded sepia and dusty rose tones, as if the map itself is slightly out of sync with time, with a few stark green accents where the Jardín de Cenizo grows."

## Mapa 7 — Continente IV, Las Fauces sin Fin (inhabitado, con estación)

"[Guía de estilo compartida]. Detailed map of a continent defined by an overwhelming density of beasts — render the landscape as ordinary rolling terrain at the edges, but increasingly dense with small beast-silhouette icons (overlapping, swarm-like) toward the center, until the central zone is rendered almost entirely as a mass of creature shapes rather than visible ground.

- Mark the single small permanent station at the safest outer margin, as far as possible from the dense center, with hunting-party route icons radiating outward but never reaching the core.
- Mark **El Dormido de las Fauces** not as a separate creature icon but as the implied source at the dead center — a vast, barely-visible colossal silhouette beneath the swarm, suggested by a faint outline under the layered beast-icons rather than drawn explicitly (its exact shape is unknown even to the station).
- Mark **La Costilla Floreciente** (an Ascendido Anclado growing over a partial skeleton) near the station — the only landmark that visibly filters the surrounding aggression, rendered with a calm green glow against the otherwise dense, hostile terrain.
- Color accent: dull browns and dried-blood reds toward the center, fading to a calmer green near the station; the overall impression should be of density and suffocating abundance rather than barren danger."

## Mapa 8 — Continente V, El Aliento que Asfixia (inhabitado, con estación)

"[Guía de estilo compartida]. Detailed map of a continent saturated with overflowing Eco — render the air itself as a visible hazy overlay across the whole landmass, thicker and more opaque toward the coasts and trenches, suggesting that breathing here is dangerous.

- Mark the single small permanent station built directly into **El Filtro Hueco** (a hollow, leafless Ascendido/Corrompido tree whose trunk the community lives inside) — render it as a glowing safe pocket cutting through the hazy overlay.
- Mark **El Pulmón de Cristal** (a jellyfish/aerial-coral silhouette) floating at a mid-distance from the station, with faint radiating rings suggesting its 'breathing' pattern — a visual cue station-dwellers use to judge when the air nearby is safe.
- Mark **El Devorador de Profundidades** (Leviatán, Nivel 22 Corrompido) as a colossal shape barely visible beneath the deepest offshore trench, at the far edge of the map.
- Add a few small inert-creature icons scattered near the coast, rendered slightly faded or "frozen," representing **Los Que No Despertaron**.
- Color accent: sickly yellow-green haze over muted grays, with the station's safe pocket rendered in warm amber light by contrast."

## Mapa 9 — Continente VII, El Reloj Roto (inhabitado, con estación)

"[Guía de estilo compartida]. Detailed map of a continent where time itself is unstable — render parts of the terrain with a deliberate double-exposure effect: faint ghost-outlines of the same landmarks slightly shifted, as if seen at two different moments simultaneously.

- Mark the single small permanent station, with its safe travel-routes drawn as unusually winding dotted lines (the only paths proven not to shift in time across generations of loss).
- Mark **La Grieta Floreciente** (a Corrompido crater field of flowers with faint ghostly vision-overlays) and **El Vacío Verde** (a central crater where vegetation is visible but deliberately rendered blurred/unmappable, survey markers fading at its edge).
- Mark **El Brote que Envejece al Revés** (a small plant landmark with a visible reverse-growth-cycle motif — flower, then seed, then bud, drawn in a circular sequence around it) near the station's outer perimeter as a known, marked hazard.
- Mark **La Tejedora de Niebla** (a giant spider silhouette) and **El Eco Adelantado** (a deer/bird-hybrid silhouette, rendered with a faint duplicate 'afterimage' just ahead of it) as the continent's signature creatures.
- Color accent: pale washed-out tones overall, with isolated patches of mismatched color where the time-doubling effect is strongest — visually the most disorienting map of the set."

## Notas de uso

- Genera primero el **Mapa 0** para fijar la composición/paleta general; luego los 9 regionales referenciando ese resultado como guía de estilo (muchos generadores permiten "image reference" o "style reference").
- Si el generador no soporta tanto texto/detalle en un solo render, prioriza: terreno + Anclados + las 10 ciudades + estación (en los inhabitados) en el render principal, y deja sucursales de Instituciones como íconos pequeños sin etiqueta detallada.
- Con los Mapas 1-9 quedan cubiertos los 3 continentes habitados y los 6 inhabitados nombrados hasta ahora (`Geografia_del_Mundo.md` sección 4.6). Los otros 16 continentes inhabitados siguen sin nombre — material para mapas futuros si se desarrolla su lore.
